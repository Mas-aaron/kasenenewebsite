About Our Founder

Fr. Bernard Rabwoni Akiiki, a Catholic priest of Fort Portal Diocese, ordained on 30th June 2018. His own journey through hardship and determination inspired him to create opportunities for others.

"I was always very interested in going to school because I wanted to become a priest or a lawyer. But school was more than 10 kilometers away. I walked this distance barefoot, and we had no proper buildings—just trees for classrooms. Despite poverty and many challenges, I was able to study up to PhD level. That’s why I founded this school." – Fr. Bernard

The Journey

Growing up in rural western Uganda, Fr. Bernard witnessed firsthand the struggles children face in accessing education. After becoming a priest, he was determined to make a difference.
He purchased land in Kasenene, a village in Kyegegwa District, one of Uganda's poorest regions, and began the school with makeshift wooden classrooms. The goal was clear: to build a low-cost, community school where even the poorest children could receive quality education close to home.

About the School

Cause of Our Joy Junior School - Kasenene is a nursery and primary school located in western Uganda. The school was established to provide accessible, affordable education for children in Kasenene and surrounding villages, where schools were previously more than 5 to 10 kilometers away.

The school serves children from nursery to primary level, and aims to offer more than just academics—it strives to be a nurturing space where children grow in character, faith, and confidence.

Our History

2021

School Founding

Cause of Our Joy Junior School was established on March 26, 2021 with 74 pupils (P.1 to P.4) in temporary wooden classrooms, with a vision to provide accessible education to children in Kasenene and surrounding villages.

2022

Post-COVID Reopening

The school reopened on January 10, 2022 after COVID closures with 191 students, showing strong community support despite challenges.

2023

Permanent Structures

With support from Missio Austria and Bildungshilfe für Uganda, the school began constructing permanent classroom blocks to replace temporary structures.

2025

Current Status

Today, the school serves about 280 pupils from nursery to primary level, with some classes still in temporary buildings as construction continues.

Our Journey: From Makeshift to Permanent Structures

When the school opened, learning took place in temporary wooden classrooms. But with unwavering determination, Fr. Bernard reached out for help during his studies at Vienna University. Through the generosity of Missio Austria and Bildungshilfe für Uganda and other friends of the school, we have been able to construct permanent classroom blocks.

Witness the transformation from humble makeshift shelters to our new solid structures. While some classes still operate from temporary buildings, we remain hopeful and committed to fully transitioning to permanent infrastructure.

Challenges and Hope

Most of our pupils come from very poor backgrounds, which poses challenges for school operations and staff salaries. The Kyegegwa District lacks critical services like access to clean water and quality healthcare. Despite these hardships, we press on—because we believe education is the key to breaking the cycle of poverty.

Future Plans

We have big dreams for our children and community:

  • Building dormitories for orphans and children with disabilities
  • Establishing a secondary school with vocational training
  • Providing scholarships and support programs for the most vulnerable
  • Enhancing access to clean water and basic health services
