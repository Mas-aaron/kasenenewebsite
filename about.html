Our Founder's Story

A Vision Born from Experience

Cause of Our Joy Junior School - Kasenene was founded by Fr. Bernard Rabwoni Akiiki, a Catholic priest of Fort Portal Diocese, ordained on 30th June 2018. His own journey through hardship and determination inspired him to create opportunities for others.

"I was always very interested in going to school because I wanted to become a priest or a lawyer. But school was more than 10 kilometers away. I walked this distance barefoot, and we had no proper buildings—just trees for classrooms. Despite poverty and many challenges, I was able to study up to PhD level. That's why I founded this school." - Fr. Bernard Rabwoni Akiiki

Our History

2021

School Founding

Cause of Our Joy Junior School was established on March 26, 2021 with 74 pupils (P.1 to P.4) in temporary wooden classrooms, with a vision to provide accessible education to children in Kasenene and surrounding villages.

2022

Post-COVID Reopening

The school reopened on January 10, 2022 after COVID closures with 191 students, showing strong community support despite challenges.

2023

Permanent Structures

With support from Missio Austria and Bildungshilfe für Uganda, the school began constructing permanent classroom blocks to replace temporary structures.

2025

Current Status

Today, the school serves about 280 pupils from nursery to primary level, with some classes still in temporary buildings as construction continues.

Our Challenges & Future Plans

Current Challenges

  • Many pupils come from very poor backgrounds
  • Limited resources for school operations and staff salaries
  • Some classes still in temporary buildings
  • Kyegegwa District lacks clean water and quality healthcare

Future Plans

  • Building dormitories for orphans and children with disabilities
  • Establishing a secondary school with vocational training
  • Providing scholarships for the most vulnerable
  • Enhancing access to clean water and health services
  • Completing transition to permanent infrastructure
